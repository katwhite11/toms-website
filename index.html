About

Decades in the Text,
a Decade in the Classroom

Dr. Thomas Lyons is a theologian and curriculum consultant with over two decades of teaching experience in ministry and academic contexts — including ten years in formal graduate and seminary classrooms. His work bridges rigorous academic theology and practical ministry formation, building courses that equip students to think deeply and serve faithfully.

In addition to his academic work, Thomas has served the Vineyard movement in a variety of local, national, and global ministry roles for more than 17 years. He consults with seminaries and graduate ministry programs to design theology courses, develop syllabi, and create engaging materials that meet both academic standards and the formational needs of students.

He lives in Lexington, Kentucky with his wife and their children, along with Charlie, their golden retriever.

Theology Courses for
Graduate & Seminary Programs

Fully developed, accreditation-ready courses available for licensing and adaptation.

✦

Christian Theology

A foundational survey of Christian doctrine — exploring the nature of God, Scripture, humanity, salvation, and eschatology with both academic rigor and pastoral depth.

Graduate
✦

Biblical Pneumatology

A focused biblical-theological study of the Holy Spirit across both Testaments, with particular attention to Luke-Acts and Pauline pneumatology, reflecting Dr. Lyons's own area of scholarly expertise.

Graduate / Seminary
✦

Biblical Research Methods & Academic Writing

Equipping students with the tools of biblical scholarship — from exegetical method and primary source engagement to the craft of theological argumentation and academic writing.

Graduate
✦

Teachings of Jesus in the Gospels

An in-depth examination of Jesus's teaching ministry as presented across the four Gospels, attending to genre, context, and the formation implications of his words for contemporary disciples.

Graduate / Seminary
✦

The Book of Acts

A close reading of Luke's second volume — tracing the expansion of the early church through the work of the Spirit, the mission of Paul, and the theological themes that unite Luke-Acts as a single narrative.

Graduate
✦

The Letter to the Ephesians

An exegetical and theological study of Ephesians, examining its grand vision of the church, Paul's ethical imperatives, and the letter's enduring significance for Christian formation and mission.
